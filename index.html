This type of pet shop sells supplies, food, and toys for domestic pets including dogs, cats, birds, fish, hamsters, and guinea pigs.


Why did I choose this Business?

 One of the greatest advantages in starting a pet shop business is a chance to share my animal passion to others. Typically, customers that shop local pet shops have an affinity for animals that extends beyond the minimum thereshold of tolerance. You get a chance to build a relationships with your core customers and enjoy the opportunity to discuss pet products.
Opening a small pet shop can provide an opportunity to work with animals and be your own boss, it requires plenty of effect and careful planning.

How to open a pet shop?

 Like all new business ventures, the success of your pet store depends on creating a positive atmosphere and meeting your customers needs. Selling pet supplies requires complying with regulations upholding certain standards and performing necessary procedures.

Start a Plan


 Develop a business plan that includes your purpose and goals, outlining the necessary steps to achieve your desired outcome. A good business plan includes researching the market to determine your clientele and expected profit. Include possible sources for pets and pet supplies, as well as the prices you intend to charge for high dollar items. Print out your business plan to present to your lender for consideration when seeking a business loan.

Know the Law

 State and local laws are constantly changing when it comes to the sale of pets. Research the law in your area thoroughly.

Pick a Location

 Select a suitable location for your pet store. Choose an area that provides easy access and good exposure to individuals that have pets, such as a shopping mall or an area near a dog park.

Register your business

 Register your business. After selecting a name for your pet shop, talk to an attorney about setting up your business structure. Your business structure will determine how you register it with the various levels of government, as well as impose requirements, such as offering stock options and conducting business meetings. Hire an attorney to ensure your pet shop business meets all legal requirements.
